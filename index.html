01
Animal Housing & Acclimatisation
⏱ 7 days 👥 1 FTE 📍 Animal facility
▼
House 40 male C57BL/6 mice (8 weeks old, 20–22g) in groups of 5 per cage under standard conditions: 12h light/dark cycle, 22°C ± 2°C, ad libitum access to standard chow (Altromin 1324) and sterile water. Allow 7 days acclimatisation before any intervention begins. Weigh and randomise mice into two groups of 20: treatment group (LGG supplementation) and vehicle control group (PBS gavage).
⚠ Confirm IACUC approval before ordering animals. Typical approval timeline: 3–4 weeks.
02
Probiotic Preparation & Daily Gavage
⏱ 28 days 👥 1 FTE 📍 BSL-1 lab
▼
Prepare fresh LGG suspension daily from lyophilised stock (ATCC 53103). Resuspend in sterile PBS to 1×10⁹ CFU/100µL. Administer 100µL daily via oral gavage to treatment group. Control mice receive 100µL sterile PBS by gavage on the same schedule. Monitor body weight every 3 days. Record gavage compliance and any adverse events.
📌 Critical: prepare suspension fresh daily — LGG viability drops >50% after 4h at room temperature.
03
FITC-Dextran Permeability Assay
⏱ 2 days 👥 2 FTE 📍 Fluorescence reader
▼
On Day 28, fast mice for 4h. Administer FITC-Dextran 4kDa (Sigma-Aldrich FD4, Cat# 46944) by oral gavage at 600mg/kg body weight in PBS. Collect blood via submandibular vein at 1h, 2h, and 4h post-gavage. Centrifuge at 3,000×g for 10 min, collect serum. Measure fluorescence on a microplate reader (excitation 485nm, emission 528nm) against a FITC-Dextran standard curve (0–2,000 ng/mL). Express results as serum FITC-Dextran concentration (ng/mL).
04
Tissue Collection & Fixation
⏱ 1 day 👥 2 FTE 📍 Dissection suite
▼
Euthanise mice by CO₂ asphyxiation following institutional protocol. Collect 1cm sections of proximal jejunum, mid-ileum, and proximal colon. Fix half of each section in 10% neutral buffered formalin (NBF) for 24h at RT (for IHC). Snap-freeze the other half in liquid nitrogen and store at −80°C (for Western blot). Collect cecal contents into 2mL cryotubes and snap-freeze for microbiome analysis (optional).
05
Western Blot — Claudin-1 & Occludin
⏱ 3 days 👥 1 FTE 📍 Protein lab
▼
Homogenise frozen tissue in RIPA lysis buffer (Thermo Fisher, Cat# 89900) with protease inhibitor cocktail. Determine protein concentration by BCA assay (Thermo Fisher Pierce BCA, Cat# 23225). Load 30µg protein per lane on 10% SDS-PAGE gel. Transfer to PVDF membrane (Bio-Rad). Block 1h in 5% non-fat milk/TBST. Incubate overnight at 4°C with: anti-claudin-1 (Abcam ab96060, 1:1000), anti-occludin (Abcam ab216327, 1:500), anti-β-actin (loading control, Abcam ab8226, 1:2000). Secondary: HRP-conjugated anti-rabbit (1:5000). Develop with ECL reagent.
📌 Use 10% SDS-PAGE gel for claudin-1 (~23kDa) and occludin (~65kDa) — note: prior protocol versions used 12% incorrectly (scientist correction applied ✓)
06
Statistical Analysis
⏱ 3 days 👥 1 FTE 📍 R / GraphPad Prism
▼
Analyse FITC-Dextran data using unpaired two-tailed Student's t-test (n=20 per group). Calculate % reduction from control mean. Western blot band intensities quantified with ImageJ, normalised to β-actin. Express data as mean ± SEM. Statistical significance at p<0.05. Power analysis: n=20 gives 85% power to detect ≥30% reduction (α=0.05, assumed SD=20% based on published data).
🔀 Alternative approach
If oral gavage compliance is low or FITC assay shows high baseline variability, consider Ussing chamber electrophysiology (TEER measurement) as an orthogonal permeability measure. Requires ~$8,000 additional equipment but provides more mechanistic insight into paracellular transport.

🎯 Primary endpoint
Serum FITC-Dextran concentration (ng/mL) at 4h post-gavage on Day 28. Success criterion: ≥30% reduction in mean serum FITC-Dextran in the LGG group vs PBS control (p<0.05, unpaired t-test, n=20/group). Power: 85% at α=0.05 assuming SD of 20% based on published murine gut permeability data.
📊 Secondary endpoints
(1) Western blot band intensity of claudin-1 and occludin normalised to β-actin — expect ≥1.5× increase in LGG group.

(2) Body weight trajectory throughout the 4-week intervention — confirm no significant difference between groups (p>0.05) to rule out confounding effects on permeability.

(3) IHC staining of tight junction proteins in jejunum cross-sections — qualitative confirmation of WB findings.
